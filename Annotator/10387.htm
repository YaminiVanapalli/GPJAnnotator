Thomson Learning: Program strategy to drive consistency and awareness

 

Situation

Thomson Learning has grown through acquisitions to become a leading learning solutions provider to corporate, academic and government organizations. Their challenge now lies in building brand awareness for its Learning Solutions positioning at the same time as it integrated and aligned each of its 12 companies around its single-brand focused value proposition. This had to be done while still providing some autonomy for each business to sell products and address its niche customer needs.

 

Solution

Events, and especially tradeshows, remain the largest single marketing investment for the collective companies and was therefore the logical place to begin marketing integration efforts. Thomson turned to GPJ to assist in this activity through a series of day-long workshops to educate its marketing and event managers in each company on the process and practice of Integrated Event Marketing.

 

Working with Thomson executives, GPJ helped define corporate marketing priorities of building brand awareness and improving cross company integration in the service of its value proposition. Then each company workshop was focused on analyzing its event portfolio using a consistent set of tools and benchmarks. Following the portfolio analysis activity was a series of Experience Engineering workshops designed to help build a consistent and shared message foundation. This was followed by the design of a measurement program to track future event performance and every element was outlined in a comprehensive Event Marketing Guidelines document.

 

Results

The Thomson Event Marketing Guidelines have helped shrink the total event portfolio and budget with a corresponding increase in total program performance. Adoption of the program has also lead to more consistent and focused branding and execution in over 200 events annually. (Not to mention more clarity across divisions and product lines).
