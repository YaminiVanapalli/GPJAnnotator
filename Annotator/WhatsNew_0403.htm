New Graphic Approval Interface…

A brand new Graphic Approval interface is now available for reviewing and approving Graphics online.  The interface allows you to filter your view to include only the files waiting for your review.  Or, through a brand new Search feature, you can find a specific set of Graphics, either by a Keyword search, or by searching based on file data (ie:  Poster, posting date, file type, file status, Client, Approver, etc.).  The new interface also provides a means to associate a Graphic Approver from you team to any Graphic file (if desired). 

 

And, to make access even easier:  When a Graphic file is posted via the Graphic Importer, an automated email is sent out to the Approvers, complete with a direct-access Hyperlink that takes you to the login, and then directly to the file.  And with the new “Send-A-Link” feature, you can email any Annotator user a hyperlink that will allow them to easily access a file you want them to review.  Also, a new “Settings…” option allows you to store a default Client that will automatically be selected when you enter either the Graphics Handler or the Space Plan Viewer.

 

GPJ Digital Image Library…

The GPJ Digital Image Library is an internal tool available to all GPJ users.  The image files stored on the Design and Marketing drives are now accessible online from the Annotator.  A new link on the startup menu will take you directly to a myriad of project photos and sample graphics that are downloadable and printable.  An icon will display when Case Studies are available for the selected image.  These Case Study documents provide a means to cut & paste bulleted points for your use.  A robust Search tool will help you to locate the images you are looking for via the new associated Keywords option, or through an advanced search of file attributes.

 

Exciting Space Planning Changes…

You’ll soon be noticing that all of the emailed Space Plan notifications include a direct-access hyperlink.  These links will take you to the login and then directly to the Floorplan it references.  The next step (coming soon!) will bypass the login.  And, while looking at a plan, you now have the ability to “Send-A-Link” to another Annotator user from the viewer (or right-click) menu.

 

But… The most exciting Hyperlink feature is the new Space Plan Hyperlinks.  Now, as you hover your mouse over the plan, you will discover embedded hyperlinks in all the newly posted Space Plans.  The cursor will change into a hand as you approach a display on the plan.  Click on it, and a menu pops up offering you the ability to view digital photos of the Element, or its associated Graphics.  The hyperlink can also take you to a display of the Element’s recent usage over the past year and all of its future assignments.  It also links you to its Inventory data.  There’s lots of stuff to discover.

 

As before, all Space Plans are printable and downloadable (as either a PDF or a DWF).  Or, you can email a PDF version to a non-Annotator user, if needed.

 

Streamlined Graphics Handler Interface…

The Graphics Handler interface has gone through several changes, making it much easier to use.  As touched on above, there is a brand new Search tool that will help you locate specific Graphic files quickly.  Also, two of the former view options have been combined into one:  “Show Season Graphics.”  The tab displays a full season of shows (with a sort option to display alphabetically or chronologically) with separate folders for “Show Specific Graphics” and either “Assigned Element Graphics”,  or “Archived Element Graphics” (which is a snapshot of Element Graphics after a show has closed).  Check it out.

 

Also, by request, Graphics can now be placed in a named folder when being imported in the Graphic Importer.  These folders make it much easier to navigate on the “Client Graphics” tab.

 

Other features include a new “Send-A-Link” option, which allows you to email a direct-access hyperlink to a fellow Annotator user.  One click takes them to the login, then directly to the file.  (We’re working on bypassing the login, which is coming soon!)  All files are downloadable, printable, and searchable.
